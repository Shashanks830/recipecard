Masala Vegetable Khichdi

A hearty, one-pot comfort meal with rice, lentils, and aromatic spices.
🥣 Serves 2 ⏱️ 45 Mins 🔥 Medium Spice

Ingredients

The Base
  • 50g Short Grain Starchy Rice
  • 25g Pulses/Dal (Starchy variety)
  • Cooking Oil (Ghee or Mustard Oil)
  • 2 tbsp Yogurt
  • 250-300ml Water (approx. 3x volume)
Fresh Produce
  • 1 Onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 Small Potato, cubed
  • 1 Carrot, chopped
  • ½ cup Peas
  • ½ cup Cauliflower/Broccoli
  • 1 Tomato, mixed in
  • Green or Red Chillies, whole
Spice Box
  • 1 tsp Ginger Garlic Paste
  • ½ tbsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tbsp Coriander Powder
  • ½ tbsp Cumin Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chilli Powder (Hot)
  • 1 tsp Black Pepper (Optional)
  • Salt to taste
The Tadka (Tempering)
  • 1 tbsp Butter or Oil
  • Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • Garlic Cloves, roughly chopped

Cooking Process

01. Prep & Soak
Wash the rice (50g) and pulses (25g) thoroughly. Let them soak in water while you prepare the vegetables. Chop all vegetables; keep hard vegetables (potatoes, carrots) separate from softer ones (cauliflower, broccoli).
02. The Aromatics
Heat oil (or ghee) in a deep vessel or pressure cooker (at least 10cm depth) on medium flame. Add sliced onions and green chillies. Sauté until onions turn light brown. Add a pinch of salt to help onions release moisture.
03. Sauté Vegetables
Phase 1: Add hard vegetables (Carrots, Potatoes). Sauté for 1 min.
Phase 2: Add softer vegetables (Cauliflower, Broccoli). Sauté for 1 min.
Phase 3: Add peas and tomatoes. Sauté for 2 mins.
04. The Masala Base
Add 1 tsp ginger garlic paste and mix well. Lower flame to medium-high. Add the spices in this specific order:
Turmeric Coriander Cumin Red Chilli Black Pepper

Add 2 tbsp Yogurt. If the mixture sticks, splash a little water. Sauté until the spices release their oil (approx. 60-70% cooked).
05. The Main Cook
Add the soaked Rice and Pulses. Mix to coat grains in masala. Add roughly 3 times the quantity of water (250-300ml). Adjust salt to taste.
Pressure Cooker: Cook for 3 whistles.
Open Pot: Cover and cook for at least 25 minutes until soft.
06. The Final Tempering (Tadka)
In a separate small ladle/pan, heat butter or oil. Add Cumin seeds and chopped Garlic. Wait until garlic turns golden brown.

Pour this hot oil into the cooked Khichdi and immediately close the lid to trap the smoky flavor (you should hear a sizzling sound!). Leave for 1 minute before serving.
Made with ❤️ · Best served hot with a side of pickle or papadum.
